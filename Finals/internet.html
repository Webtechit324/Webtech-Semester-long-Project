Internet

- a global system of interconnected computer networks that use the standard Internet Protocol Suite (TCP/IP) to serve billion of users worldwide.

- it is a networks that consists of millions of private, public, academic, business and government networks of local to global scope, that are linked by a broad array of electronic, wireless and optical networking technologies.

Vinton Cerf

- father of the Internet

- he is a co-designer of the TCP/IP and the architecture of the Internet

History of the Internet

October 24 1995

- Internet means a global information system that is logically linked by a globally unique address space based on the internet protocol (IP).

- Transmission Control Protocol (TCP) means sending information over the internet and this provides high level services publicly or privately. This is also a platform that create higher level services for users.

1969 (established)

- four computers (that talks to one another) connected to the Advance Research Project Agency Network (ARPANET) by Defense Advanced Research Project Agency (DARPA).

1972

- electronic mail (e-mail) - the first killer application was deploy on the web.
