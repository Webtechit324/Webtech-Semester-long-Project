World Wide Web

- WWW 1989

- is a combination of all resources and users on the Internet that are using the Hypertext Transfer Protocol (HTTP).

- the term is often mistakenly used as a synonym for the Internet itself, but the web is a service that operates over the Internet, as electronic mail (e-mail) does.

Tim Berners-Lee

- father of the web

- in September 1994, Lee founded the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology with support from the Defense Advanced Research Project Agency (DARPA) and the European Commision. It comprised various companies that were willing to create standards and recommend adaptations to improve the quality of the web.

- made the Hypertext Transfer Protocol (HTTP), Hypertext Markup Language (HTML), Unified Resource Locator (URL), Web Server, Web Client and Hyperlinking of documents.

Wide Area Information Servers (WAIS)

- periodically queries databases and get the result and prints what you needed.
